Frequently Asked Questions

When are the results visible?

The response to stem cell therapy varies depending on the treatment area and condition. Initial improvements may appear within one week, while visible results are typically seen within 1-3 months. Regeneration continues for up to a year, ensuring natural and long-lasting effects.

How is the Cell Therapy Center different from others?

We stand out through our advanced technologies, internationally compliant facilities, and experienced medical team. We offer both autologous and allogeneic stem cell therapy, ensuring an individualized approach for every patient.

What is stem cell therapy?

Stem cell therapy (SCT) is an innovative treatment approach using the patient’s own or umbilical cord-derived stem cells to repair damaged tissues.

It is applied in both aesthetic and medical contexts.

Stem cells can be obtained from adipose tissue, umbilical cord or bone marrow.

They have been used in medicine since 1988 for the treatment of more than 80 diseases, including neuromuscular and degenerative disorders.

In recent years, thanks to advances in scientific research and new technologies, cell therapy has become one of the most promising directions in modern medicine.

Degenerative disorders occur as a result of the wear and degeneration of bones, cartilage, muscles, or other tissues. The most common cause is natural aging. These disorders develop slowly but later become chronic and limiting.

The most common degenerative diseases include osteoarthritis, chronic renal and heart failure, myocardial infarction, as well as neurological disorders such as Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s diseases.

Stem cells are undifferentiated cells capable of becoming specialized cells. They can be directed toward specific cell lineages, allowing physicians to use them for tissue repair. After injection, stem cells migrate to the site of injury and transform into the types of cells required for tissue regeneration or healing.

How does stem cell therapy work?

Mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) repair damaged areas, but in individuals with severe injury or degenerative disease, the body may lack enough functional cells or fail to mobilize them efficiently.

Research shows that MSCs can restore damaged tissue, modulate the immune response, reduce inflammation, and secrete growth factors that promote healing while suppressing chronic inflammation.

Key properties of MSCs include:

Stimulation of regeneration

Potent anti-inflammatory effects

Immune system activation

Protection against premature cell and tissue damage

How are stem cells administered?

The method depends on the condition and its severity. Stem cells can be injected locally into the damaged or painful area, subcutaneously or intravenously.

Osteoarthritis and Joint Problems

Stem Cell Therapy for Osteoarthritis

The goal of stem cell therapy for osteoarthritis is to stimulate regeneration, improve mobility, and enhance the patient’s quality of life.

Why do patients with osteoarthritis choose stem cell therapy instead of surgery?

Stem cell transplantation promotes joint regeneration.

It is less invasive compared to surgery.

The therapy relies on the body’s natural healing mechanisms.

Patients recover faster after stem cell therapy.

Stem cell therapy can help patients with osteoarthritis by improving mobility, reducing inflammation, and potentially preventing or delaying the need for surgical intervention.

What is Osteoarthritis?

Osteoarthritis is the most common joint disease. It develops when the cartilage that cushions the joints wears down, causing bones to rub against each other.

This leads to pain, swelling, stiffness, and reduced mobility. Over time, it may cause muscle weakness and ligament instability.

The most frequently affected joints are the knees, hips, spine, and hands.

Why Consider Stem Cell Therapy?

Surgery is effective in severe cases but carries risks and requires a long recovery period sometimes up to a year.

Possible complications of surgical intervention:

Blood clots

Pulmonary embolism

Infection

Nerve or tissue injury

Implant fracture

Heart attack

Osteolysis (reaction to metal or plastic implants)

Bleeding and pain

Immediately after stem cell therapy, the patient can return to normal daily life.

How Does Stem Cell Therapy Improve Osteoarthritis Symptoms?

Stem cell therapy represents an innovative approach to cartilage regeneration in osteoarthritis. The disease’s pathogenesis involves cartilage degeneration, bone structure changes, and chronic synovial inflammation.
